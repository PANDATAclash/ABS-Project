 Introduction 
1.1 Background 
In severe motorcycle accidents, skid marks created by a locked front wheel are often present. These marks provide valuable information for forensic investigators, particularly for estimating the initial speed of the motorcycle prior to braking. 
Currently, it is difficult to safely reproduce such locked-wheel braking scenarios under controlled conditions. Traditional stabilizing solutions, such as side wheels, interfere with the normal force acting on the front tire, which directly affects braking force and measurement accuracy.  
The Police Academy has therefore initiated this project to develop a dedicated support system that enables realistic braking experiments while ensuring rider safety. 
 
1.2 Project Objective 
The objective of this project is to design and realize a motorcycle support system that does the following:  
Preventing the motorcycle from falling during front-wheel lock-up. It allows controlled lean to reproduce characteristic tire marks 
Maintaining a constant normal force on the front wheel 
Enables repeated deceleration measurements 
The resulting system will be used to determine tire-road friction values and assist in accident reconstruction. 
 
1.3 Stakeholders 
Client: Police Academy – Forensic Traffic Investigation 
End users: Dutch forensic traffic investigators 
Technical supervisors: HAN Automotive Research 
 
 
 
2. Problem Definition 
When a motorcycle’s front wheel locks during braking, the vehicle becomes unstable and typically falls. For testing purposes, a support mechanism is required. 
Currently existing stabilizing solutions introduce two major problems: 
They restrict motorcycle lean, preventing realistic tire mark generation 
They reduce front wheel normal force when auxiliary wheels contact the ground, lowering braking force and corrupting measurements 
This creates a design contradiction: 
The motorcycle must be supported, but that support must not influence braking physics. 
 
3. Project Requirements 
Functional Requirements (MoSCoW) 

Must 
Safe operation during locked front wheel braking 
Ability to brake for at least 2 seconds with locked front wheel 
No change in front-wheel normal force due to the support system 
Use of a modern motorcycle representative of Dutch traffic 
Should 
ABS system switchable on/off 
Ability to reproduce characteristic tire marks 
Could 
Modular system usable on multiple motorcycle types
